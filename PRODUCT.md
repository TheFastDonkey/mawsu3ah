# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Arabic readers, students, researchers, and scholars looking for trusted physical editions of Arabic books. They use the site on laptops, phones, and older devices, often on slow networks. Their context is task-oriented: find a book, compare editions, submit a correction, or approve a contribution.

## Product Purpose

Build a community-driven catalog of the best physical editions of Arabic books. Editions are submitted by contributors, reviewed by admins, and ranked by authenticated user likes. Expert contributors are visually highlighted so readers can spot authoritative contributions at a glance.

## Brand Personality

Islamic, Scholarly, Not-heavy

- **Islamic**: Geometric restraint, clean rhythm, respect for Arabic typography and RTL reading.
- **Scholarly**: Trust, accuracy, and quiet hierarchy. The interface feels like a well-kept reference room, not a marketplace.
- **Not-heavy**: Light pages, minimal JavaScript, fast loads. No ornamental excess.

## Anti-references

- AI-generated or generic template aesthetic.
- Overly ornate Islamic clichés (heavy arabesque borders, gold gradients, decorative flourishes).
- Cluttered forum-style interfaces with competing borders and badges.
- Generic Bootstrap / Material admin look.
- Dense SaaS dashboards with metric cards and chart noise.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration.** Every element earns its place by helping the reader find or judge an edition.
2. **Respect Arabic reading rhythm.** RTL is default; line length, spacing, and alignment follow Arabic prose.
3. **Trust through restraint.** Subdued color, consistent components, and visible moderation state make the catalog feel reliable.
4. **Lightweight by default.** Pages stay fast and usable on weak devices and slow networks.
5. **State is visible.** Pending, approved, rejected, expert, and liked states are readable without explanation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA.
- Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Maintain contrast for body text and form placeholders.
